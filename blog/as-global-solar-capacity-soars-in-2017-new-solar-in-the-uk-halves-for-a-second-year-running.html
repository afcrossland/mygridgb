21 of the 28 EU markets showed growth, but this wasn’t enough to compensate for UK losses resulting from subsidy cuts in 2015 and 2016.

James Watson, chief executive of SolarPowerEurope: “Solar power has been voted the most popular energy source in the UK, it is therefore sad to see the UK government not take advantage of the huge potential of solar.”

Sad is not the word I would use. More like bewilderingly myopic, and a tragic testament to the their delusional addiction to the twin imminent train wrecks that are nuclear renaissance and shale production.
