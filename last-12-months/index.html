Monthly average electricity generation by fuel type and carbon intensity. Use the time window buttons to zoom in on recent months or zoom out for a longer view — seasonal patterns in wind, solar and demand become clear across the full range.

Generation mix share

Average contribution of each fuel type to the electricity mix over the selected period. Tile size reflects share of total generation — the balance between gas, nuclear, wind and solar shifts significantly across seasons and weather.

Monthly generation

Average power output (GW) by fuel type for each month. Winter months typically see higher overall demand and more wind; summer months bring solar peaks but lower total generation. Gas acts as the primary balancing fuel, rising when renewables are low.

Monthly carbon intensity

Average carbon intensity of British electricity (gCO₂eq/kWh) for each month. Carbon intensity falls when wind and solar output is high, and rises when gas fills a larger share of demand. Comparing months highlights the interplay between weather, season and grid emissions.
