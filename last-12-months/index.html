Electricity generation (TWh) by fuel type per month. Winter months typically see higher total generation and more wind; summer brings solar peaks. Gas acts as the primary balancing fuel, rising when renewables are low.

Monthly carbon intensity

Average carbon intensity of British electricity (gCO₂eq/kWh) for each month. Carbon intensity falls when wind and solar output is high and rises when gas fills a larger share of demand.

Conversion & grid losses

Not all energy becomes electricity. Gas turbines convert around 57% of fuel into power; nuclear reactors around 35%; biomass stations around 39%. The rest escapes as waste heat. A further 6% is lost in transmission once electricity reaches the grid. The diagram shows total TWh over the last 12 months — every unit of energy from source to delivered electricity.
