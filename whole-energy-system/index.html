How energy flows, and what electrifying it would do

Primary energy on the left flows through the fuels that make electricity, to the useful energy actually delivered on the right - broken out by end use - with everything else lost as waste heat. The losses are huge for road engines (~75%) and power stations (~50%), small for boilers (~10%). Values in terawatt hours (TWh).

Use the sliders to swap petrol and diesel cars for electric ones, and gas and oil boilers for heat pumps. Both are far more efficient than burning fuel, so primary energy and waste heat fall sharply. Here the extra electricity is met by scaling up today's mix of nuclear, wind, solar and gas in proportion, so only part of it adds emissions. Homes stay just as warm and cars drive just as far.

Combustion CO2 only, Great Britain. Gas here is heating & industry (power-station gas sits in electricity); oil is scaled from UK to GB. Efficiencies: boilers 90%, engines 25%, industry 75%, heat pumps SCOP 3, EVs 85%, gas power 50%. Electricity fuels use the physical-energy-content convention. Full method on the About page.
